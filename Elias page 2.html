Computer Information Systems

A Bachelor of Computer Information Systems (BCIS) discipline will teach you the necessary skills you need to build networks, provide data security and maintain an environment where computer applications run smoothly. This discipline prepares you for work in fields like computer information systems design and systems analysis in both industry and government.

A Bachelor of Computer Information Systems (CIS) discipline qualifies applicants to begin a career in the field of information technology and management. With business and government relying more and more on the flow of information through computers and online, people trained in the management and development of Computer Information Systems are in high demand.
